# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

People who find this link: recruiters briefly, but mostly curious people, other students, people from the internet. They arrive wondering who this person is, not looking for a resume. The job is to make them feel like they just discovered someone interesting.

## Product Purpose

Personal portfolio for a 2nd-year Mathematical Physics student at Waterloo who builds things from scratch (ML in C, cryptography, embedded systems). Not a resume site. A small corner of the internet that reflects an actual person: quiet, cerebral, reads Vagabond and Blame!, plays Destiny 2, cares about first principles.

## Brand Personality

Reserved. Precise. Genuine.

The voice is calm and direct, like someone who thinks before speaking. Not trying to impress, just being honest. The design should feel like a room that belongs to someone specific, not a template that could belong to anyone.

## Anti-references

- Template portfolio syndrome: cards, tag badges, ArrowUpRight icons, "Software engineer building things on the web"
- LinkedIn resume: buzzwords, action verbs, bullet-point achievements
- Dark-mode SaaS: glassmorphism, gradient text, neon, hero metrics
- Minimalism cosplay: so stripped it has no identity, could be anyone

## Design Principles

1. **First principles over abstraction.** Like the code, strip to what's necessary. No decoration that doesn't carry meaning.
2. **Specific over general.** Every word and design choice should be something only this person would make. Nothing interchangeable.
3. **Let content breathe.** The design serves reading, not the other way around.
4. **Quiet confidence.** No hover effects that shout. No badges that brag. Presence through restraint.
5. **Dark in tone, not in theatrics.** The aesthetic leans toward weight and seriousness, not edginess.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Dark/light toggle. Reduced motion respected. No color-only meaning.
